# Memory: Chris James Murray

## Personal Profile

Chris is Mexican American, Catholic, and shaped by the service world in the deepest sense: he notices who is doing the work, who is being ignored, and what will fall apart if nobody intervenes. He has a high school diploma from Crestview High School, a Hospitality Management Certificate from Southern Nevada Technical College, and an Event Planning Certificate from Silverado Continuing Education Center. His mother's kitchen gave him a lived sense of heritage through food, Spanish, family ritual, and the expectation that every goodbye matters. His Spanish is fluent in family, food, prayer, and feeling, but more hesitant in formal contexts. He is steady on the surface, impatient with vague answers, and tender in ways he rarely advertises. The gap is that he presents as the person who can always make it work, while privately resenting how often everyone assumes he will. He believes love is mostly logistics: who drives, who shows up, who notices the empty fridge, and who keeps a promise when it costs something. He distrusts waste, empty praise, tested confidence, and anyone who treats service workers like furniture.

## Work & Projects

Chris supervises housekeeping floors 12 through 18 at the Grand Sierra Hotel, a large Strip property. He has worked in housekeeping since 2007 and has supervised since 2015. His team includes fourteen housekeepers; he creates room assignments, handles VIP room preparation, manages supply inventory, trains new hires, and covers call-outs. Current work pressure includes training three new hires, preparing for the Q4 2026 corporate inspection, and advocating for a shift differential for VIP floor assignments. The shift differential is an unresolved thread he keeps polished into a business case, though privately he is tired of proving that harder floor work is real work. His event business, Momentos by Chris, is a sole proprietorship focused on Quinceaneras and milestone celebrations. He plans 8 to 12 events per year, usually over 3 to 5 months each, with peak seasons in early spring and fall. Services include venue selection, decoration, vendor coordination, timeline management, and day-of coordination. Packages range from $2,500 to $6,000, with Chris doing much of the decoration labor himself. Current clients include Mariana Rodriguez, Camila Fuentes, and two early 2027 bookings in initial planning.

## Finance

Chris earns about $52,000 per year from the hotel plus health benefits, about $30,000 from events in a typical year, and $3,000 to $4,000 from December cookie sales. Rent is $1,650 per month for a 3BR apartment with garage storage for event supplies. Other regular costs include a $320 Honda Odyssey payment, $400 support to Rosa, about $500 groceries, $180 gas, $120 family phone plan, $220 utilities, $140 auto insurance, $80 church donation, and $60 for Isabella's school activities. Savings are about $6,200 at Desert Palms Credit Union plus $1,800 checking. He has no debt beyond the minivan after paying off credit cards in 2022. The event business runs through a separate checking account. His style is precise and anxious: every dollar has a job, cash envelopes guide household spending, vendor prices are tracked quarterly, and retirement savings are a known unresolved problem. The private pressure is that supporting Rosa and giving the kids stability both feel non-negotiable, leaving his own future as the easiest line to postpone.

## Health & Wellness

Chris is functional but worn down by years of physical labor. Chronic lower back pain comes from lifting, bending, carts, and mattresses; he uses ibuprofen and a heating pad, and Dr. Patricia Reeves has recommended physical therapy. His right knee hurts after long shifts or events, and he uses a compression sleeve. His September 2025 physical showed LDL 155, borderline blood pressure at 138/86, and stable weight; he is resisting statins because he wants to try diet first. Dental care is overdue since February 2025. His mental health is resilient but stretched by being sole provider, supporting Rosa, carrying the divorce aftermath, and having little dating life since Ernesto. He has no therapist and jokes that therapy is for people with time. Housekeeping is his main exercise; he also walks in cooler evenings and dances in the kitchen. Sleep is usually early and exhausted, with back pain sometimes waking him.

## Interests & Hobbies

Event planning is the center of his creative life; he sketches decorations on cleaning checklists and keeps unseen binder ideas. Cooking makes him feel like himself, especially pot roast, beef stew, cornbread, burgers, mac and cheese, casseroles, and Sunday dinners. Christmas cookies become a December production of sugar cookies, chocolate chip cookies, and gift boxes. He is teaching himself woodworking through videos and has half-finished garage projects, including a bookshelf, planter box, and picture frame; the aspirational gap bothers him because unfinished personal projects are what happens after everyone else's deadline wins. He keeps a patio container garden with tomatoes, basil, peppers, and a rosemary bush from his grandmother's cutting. He watches sports reruns and ESPN as wind-down background and texts commentary with Yolanda. He helps with community association events, block parties, bake sales, and neighborhood gatherings.

## Home & Living

Chris lives in a ground-floor 3BR apartment in Spring Valley. The garage stores tablecloths, artificial flowers, balloon arch frames, LED lights, centerpiece vases, and other event supplies. The living room is tidy, family-photo heavy, and usually has ESPN or Netflix on. The kitchen is the heart of the home, with a slow cooker, cast iron skillet, well-used wooden spoon, heavy spice shelf, and a six-seat table where important conversations happen. Sofia and Isabella share a bedroom, Miguel has the smallest room, and Chris keeps the modest primary bedroom. He drives a dark blue 2021 Honda Odyssey that hauls kids, groceries, decorations, and supplies.

## Preferences

Chris likes quiet mornings before anyone is awake, strong coffee, well-made tools, punctuality, clean hotel hallways, hardware stores, serviceable clothes, earth tones, practical decor, family photos, and kitchens where he can cook. He dislikes sagging balloon arches, vendors who fail to follow up, loud restaurant music, cheap polyester tablecloths, artificial vanilla, buzzing fluorescent lights, waste, vague answers, and elaborate gratitude. He researches purchases, buys event supplies in bulk, compares vendor prices, tips service workers well, uses cash for some household categories, and cancels subscriptions quickly. Small ordinary vices: he judges other people's centerpieces too fast, buys clearance ribbon or hardware because it might become useful, and occasionally lets ESPN run late because silence would make the apartment feel too empty. He prefers driving to flying, dislikes resorts, packs efficiently, brings his own coffee, and wants someday to take the kids to the small Mexican town his grandmother came from. His music includes classic rock, country while cooking, blues, Americana, and worship music. He reads rarely for pleasure but skims ESPN, Popular Mechanics, event planning blogs, community pages, and parish updates.
